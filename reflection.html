Reflection essay

Learning to code

Oslo, a big maze

We are all from different small cities around Norway. We are used to small villages where we all know each other. Classes were small, we knew everybody there, and we usually knew their whole family as well. It was just a small community, a bubble in a way. When we moved to a bigger city to a university the difference struck us momentarily. Our small classes were no longer small, we didn’t know anybody, and our small “bubble” was broken. For me the transitioning was kind of rough. I went from knowing everyone in the class of twenty people, to not knowing one, in a classroom of hundreds of students. The new university was huge compared to the old school. Normally I would know what classroom I should get to, now the hallways felt like big mazes and the huge auditoriums felt like a scene.After a while all the new things became the new normal. The huge classes weren't that big, the endless hallways, were no longer mazes, hundreds of unknown people started to be known and the auditoriums were no longer big scenes.

Learning in a new way

When we all became a bit more comfortable in the big city, we really started to focus on the courses we had at school. Most of them contained a form of programming, either html, CSS or java. Some of us had tried to learn some basics of python a few months before starting at this program, but it was a bit hard, so we thought we would get to really learn when we started at a proper programming course.But our main problem was how we didn’t really have a plan on learning all this new stuff, we didn’t really know how learning in a big university work. You watch some lectures, do some assignments, read on your own and basically do everything yourself. I think many of us weren't ready for being fully in charge of our own learning. We just didn’t expect such a big change from our upper- secondary school to the university.As we came into the rhythm of the learning format, we learned more and more about programming. And we started to value how we were in charge of everything we did. We could fully control our day- to-day life, and spend time on things we needed to do, or some days we could do anything we would like to do.

Programming

As we started to program, it became more and more difficult. Some assignments looked impossible at the start, but when we tried different things, we started to understand more. Small changes could make or break a big project. When we had new projects, we instantly tried to experiment with the code we had available. Because everything is essentially learned by trial and error.
